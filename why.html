Why Passionfruit

My journey into wellness was initially fuelled by fear and judgement. I was constantly afraid of eating the wrong things due to a misguided belief that carbohydrates in any form would feed my illness. I was frequently experiencing anxiety because I felt that I should have taken my health seriously sooner; this created hyper-vigilance and a resentment for my ‘old self’ and her ‘bad habits’. So, my ‘new self’ exercised and meditated compulsively - dedicating all of my time and energy into improving myself. Yet, I denied myself the pleasure of actually feeling good.

If I consumed something that tasted too sweet, I would become triggered. It was a vicious cycle that lasted for years.

Of course, I reached a breaking point - it was inevitable; and it happened while travelling. As I was away from my routines, it was easier to cave and to indulge in a bar of candy (Butterfingers). After years without anything sweeter than a blueberry gracing my taste buds, the ultra sweet processed sugar tasted truly sickening; but I still ate the whole damn thing in an act of self flagellation.

It was during that moment that I realised I was fragmented; that I was denying the parts of myself that really needed acknowledgment and compassion. Since this incident, I’ve forged a gentler path as I learned then that fear around food can cause more damage to the body than the food itself. It is the belief about how your body will respond to certain foods that can create those responses.

This is why Passionfruit feels radical to me. Religious paradigms demonize pleasure and have associated sweet fruits with testing temptation. These belief patterns around virtue and worthiness still permeate into the most well intentioned wellness and spiritual communities. So, as a recovering ‘virtue junkie’, I now choose to view the very existence of fruit as evidence that life is meant to be enjoyed. Identifying with Passionfruit is an acceptance of the sweet and tangy and an invitation for myself to be with it all.
