What Is Acupuncture?

Acupuncture is an ancient Chinese practice based on the belief that the body is comprised of a complex network of energy channels, known as “meridians”; vital energy, referred to as “Qi”, flows through these meridians like a highway leading to elevated Qi. Disruptions or stagnation in the flow of energy along these highways can cause dis-ease in the body. Acupuncture is a Traditional Chinese Medicine (T.C.M) that is believed to stimulate & enhance the flow of Qi while simultaneously restoring overall balance and promoting optimal functioning.

To grasp this concept, think of it like this: any injury to the body will disrupt its usual mode of functioning. As a result, the immune system will respond by sending white blood cells to the damaged area to create inflammation and to promote self-healing. This process requires energy. By creating minor perforations in the skin at strategic points along the highway, this process is intentionally induced as to influence the flow of energy in the most life affirming direction.

Receiving acupuncture can last between 60 to 90 minutes and will feel similar to a gentle (internal) massage. Most patients report feeling extremely relaxed during and after the session; some can even sense shift and flow of internal energy during the session.

Benefits of Acupuncture

A list of conditions that can be treated with acupuncture include but are not limited to:

  • Allergies
  • Asthma
  • Anxiety, depression, and other mental health conditions
  • Chronic pain, such as neck pain, back pain, and joint pain
  • High blood pressure
  • Insomnia and other sleep disorders
  • Infertility
  • Migraines and/ or chronic headaches
  • Menstrual cramps and other gynecological issues
